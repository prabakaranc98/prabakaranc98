---

I pursue intelligence as an emergent technical — and existential — phenomenon.

I don't build demos. I run experiments. I don't collect papers. I attack hypotheses.
The work compounds: every sprint sharpens the next question. Every failure is evidence. Everything is public.

What drives me: understanding *how machines actually learn and reason*, not just getting them to output something plausible.
My territory sits at the intersection of **world models, causal inference, interpretability, continual learning, and cognitive science** — areas where the interesting science and the hard engineering are the same problem.

Learning > knowing. Fundamentals are earned through execution.

## Personal Initiatives

Programs I run independently. Each one reflects a different way I think about how to learn, build, and contribute.

---

**[how-to-do-ai-research-2026](https://github.com/prabakaranc98/how-to-do-ai-research-2026)** — *Research Craft*

A public living lab on how to do serious AI/ML research. Not a tutorial. A working methodology — research sprints, honest ablation logs, and published artifacts. The premise: most people learn to build things but never learn to think through problems. This is my attempt to fix that for myself, in public.

---

**[nthExperiment](https://github.com/prabakaranc98/MATS-Exp1)** — *Experiment Series*

A lineage of focused research sprints, each targeting one hypothesis end-to-end. Not a portfolio of side projects — a record of learning to see better. Current threads: applied interpretability, causal social agents, continual learning, world models, and probabilistic inference. Each exp is one question, one implementation, one honest result.

> MATS-Exp1 · interp-exp1-socialagents · exp1-continous-learning · RMT-Exp · ...

---

**[Thursday Learning Hours](https://github.com/prabakaranc98/Thursday-Learning-Hours)** — *Learning Series*

A self-run weekly seminar — lectures I want to exist, so I make them. Each session covers one foundation, frontier, or framework in AI/ML/DS: slides, reading list, experiments, and notes. The discipline is the point. Consistency compounds.

---

**[BroCoDDE](https://github.com/prabakaranc98/broCoDDE)** — *Knowledge Engine*

A personal knowledge engine built on one conviction: don't automate your thinking — speed up the meticulous lifecycle. Two modes: Feynman-style micro-learning (explain it until it's real) and deep synthesis (5-stage pipeline from idea to artifact). The more you use it, the richer it gets. Knowledge should compound.

---

**[Cognitive ETL](https://github.com/prabakaranc98/cognitive-ETL)** — *Knowledge Infrastructure*

Most knowledge systems over-reward input. They don't create pressure to clarify, connect, reuse, or ship. Cognitive ETL treats thinking as a pipeline: books, papers, ideas → atomic insights → linked graph → public artifacts. Human judgment does the meaning work. AI helps structure it. Automation handles the rest.

---

**[Applied AI 2026](https://github.com/prabakaranc98/applied-ai-2026)** — *Applied Course*

A problem-first compendium on applied AI. Not SOTA model demos. The actual discipline of approaching a problem, bridging research to constraints, and optimizing for both performance and systems. For people who want to build, not just follow tutorials.

---

**[AI First Editorial](https://github.com/prabakaranc98/ai-first-editorial)** — *Writing*

Writing as thinking. Stories, synthesis, and analysis on AI research, engineering, and what it means to build at the frontier. The format is editorial, not academic. The intent is to say something real.

---

**[FAIRE](https://github.com/prabakaranc98/FAIRE)** — *Frontiers in AI Research and Engineering*

Sprint-based research program at the edge of what's understood in AI. Each sprint starts with a precisely defined question and ends with a concrete result — working code, a finding, or a documented wall. Problem spaces: LLMs, mechanistic interpretability, diffusions & flows, world models, continual learning, causal representation learning, post-training & alignment, systems, and foundation model science.

Four objectives every sprint must serve: **Discovery · Evidence · Inference · Optimization**. Not sequential stages — a loop. The constraint is clarity, not novelty. The goal is to know something real by the end.
